Of Storms & Stars

A story written in the margins of the universe

In 2024, I fell into the darkness.
Six times I tried to leave this world behind —
six times the world refused to let me go.

Then, on December 27th, when the nights were cold and empty,
a miracle I never dared dream of found me.
Her name was Nafisha.
She had eyes that could silence storms,
hair that fell like soft rivers down her shoulders,
and a face so beautiful it defied all words,
as if even the stars were ashamed to shine beside her.

For a moment, I believed in something again.

School began — and with it, the weight of life returned.
We argued, our voices louder than our hearts,
but my love never wavered, even when the days grew heavy.

On Valentine’s Day, 2025,
I gave her a bouquet of flowers —
every petal carrying the hopes of a boy who had nothing left but her.
For a while, the sun came back to me.

But soon, storms rose again between us —
arguments, misunderstandings, unspoken words tearing at the thread that held us together.
We broke apart,
then ran back into each other’s arms, desperate, afraid to lose what we knew was rare.
But sometimes love isn’t enough to kill the demons we carry.

A misunderstanding —
just one —
ripped the fragile thing we built into pieces.

We were once so close — closer than words could ever describe —
and now, we are strangers.
Strangers who once knew every secret, every dream, every scar on each other's hearts.
And yet, even now, I still text her to ask how her day was,
even knowing she may never answer.
I still pray for her happiness in the quiet moments no one sees.
I still whisper her name into the silence.

It tears me apart knowing she will never be mine.
I have to watch her from afar,
seeing every beauty in nature, every song that plays, and it reminds me of her.
And no matter how many times I try to move on,
I always find pieces of her in someone else.
No one will ever compare.

They say it’s God’s plan,
but what if my only plan was her?
What if I only want her for the rest of my life?

I pray to God every day,
that in some other universe, she will be mine.
That somehow, someway,
the love we lost will find its way back to us.

I would give her my life —
gladly, without a second thought —
just to spend a few more stolen minutes with Nafisha,
to see her smile once more before fading into nothing.

And so, once again, I am left with nothing
but memories of her laugh,
the way her hair framed her perfect face,
and the ache of what could have been.
And if life was repeated a thousand times
I would still choose her
